# Intake Questions

## Round 1 — Core Inputs (Required, ask all together)

- **Target professor's research interests** (REQUIRED) — describe the professor's research areas, recent directions, and keywords (e.g., "power system resilience, physics-informed deep learning for grid optimization, renewable energy forecasting")
- **Target professor's recent publications** (REQUIRED) — list 5–10 of the professor's most relevant recent papers (last 3–5 years). For each paper, provide: title, year, venue (journal/conference), and DOI if available. This is the primary source for understanding the professor's current research trajectory and will be directly cited in the proposal. The user is responsible for verifying this information from the professor's homepage, Google Scholar, or other academic databases.
- **Your research field and specific interests** (e.g., "computer vision, specifically 3D reconstruction")
- **Target university and program name**
- **Path to your CV/resume file** (.docx, .tex, or .typ) — leave blank to describe background manually

## Round 2 — Research Interest Detail (ask after Round 1 is answered)

- **Research interest keywords**: List 3–8 keywords that best describe your research interests. These will be used to align with the professor's work and to drive the literature search.
- **Detailed research interest description**: Provide a detailed write-up of your research interests — ideally a Markdown file path (e.g., a personal research statement, a mini-review, or annotated notes). This document should describe: the problems you care about, the approaches you find promising, any preliminary ideas or hypotheses, and relevant work you have already read. If you do not have a file, paste the content directly. This is the single most important input for shaping the proposal's direction.
- **Review/survey article summaries** (REQUIRED): Provide a Markdown file (or paste content) summarizing multiple review/survey articles relevant to your research direction. This summary should cover: the field's current state, key open problems, emerging trends, important methodologies, and seminal references. This is the primary source for identifying research gaps and positioning the proposal.

## Round 3 — Framing & Compliance (ask after Round 2 is answered)

- Are there any key theories or conceptual frameworks central to your field? (e.g., "attention restoration theory", "transformer architecture", "density functional theory") — helps anchor the theoretical framework section
- Does your research involve human subjects, animal experiments, sensitive personal data, or hazardous materials? (Determines depth of the Ethical Considerations section — answer "none" if purely computational or theoretical)
- Does your target university or funding body have specific formatting requirements, word limits, or structural guidelines? (If yes, describe them — the proposal will be tailored accordingly; default is 2000–3000 words)
- Is there a specific citation style required, or should we use APA? (default: APA 7th edition)
