# **Checklist: Research Proposal**

This checklist is designed to help you develop a strong and persuasive research proposal that clearly outlines your research plan and its significance within your field. As always, specific requirements may vary per university or funding body, so it’s essential to review any briefing documents and assessment criteria provided.

### **Title and Introduction:**

- [ ] I've chosen a clear, concise, and descriptive title that reflects the core focus of my research.  
- [ ] I've provided a strong introduction that clearly articulates the research problem or question, capturing the reader's interest.  
- [ ] I've clearly stated the purpose of the research and its significance within the broader field of study.  
- [ ] I’ve articulated the research gap that my study will address.  
- [ ] I've provided background information that contextualises the research problem, setting the stage for the proposal.

### **Research Questions and Objectives:**

- [ ] I've clearly defined the research questions or hypotheses that my study will address.  
- [ ] I've outlined specific, measurable, and achievable research objectives that align with my research questions.  
- [ ] I've ensured that my research questions and objectives are logically connected and focused.

      ### 

### **Literature Review:**

- [ ] I've included a brief but comprehensive review of the relevant literature, highlighting key studies and theories related to my research topic.  
- [ ] I've identified gaps in the existing literature that my research aims to address.  
- [ ] I've demonstrated how my research will contribute to the existing body of knowledge.

### **Research Methodology:**

- [ ] I've outlined a clear and appropriate research design (e.g., qualitative, quantitative, or mixed-methods).  
- [ ] I've described the sampling method, including the sample size, population, and criteria for selection.  
- [ ] I've detailed the data collection methods, explaining how data will be gathered and any tools or instruments that will be used.  
- [ ] I've explained the data analysis techniques that will be employed to answer my research questions.  
- [ ] I’ve acknowledged potential limitations of my chosen approach.

### **Theoretical Framework (if applicable):**

- [ ] I've provided a clear explanation of the theoretical or conceptual framework that will guide my research.  
- [ ] I've explained how this framework is relevant to my research questions and how it informs my methodological choices.

### **Ethical Considerations:**

- [ ] I've identified any ethical issues related to my research and explained how they will be addressed (e.g., informed consent, confidentiality).  
- [ ] I've outlined any steps that will be taken to ensure the ethical conduct of the research.

      ### 

### **Timeline and Budget (if applicable):**

- [ ] I've provided a realistic timeline that outlines the major milestones of my research, from data collection to final analysis.  
- [ ] I've included a detailed budget (if required), justifying the allocation of resources and listing any expected expenses.

### **Significance and Impact:**

- [ ] I've clearly articulated the potential impact of my research on the field, including how it might influence theory, practice, or policy.  
- [ ] I've explained how my research will contribute to addressing the identified research gap or problem.

### **Compliance and Quality Control:**

- [ ] I've adhered to any formatting and structural guidelines specified by my university or funding body.  
- [ ] I've thoroughly edited and proofread the proposal to ensure it is free from spelling, grammar, punctuation, and typographical errors.  
- [ ] I’ve ensured that the future tense is used for writing the proposal.   
- [ ] I've ensured that all sources are completely and correctly cited and referenced according to the required citation style.  
- [ ] I've confirmed that the writing is clear, concise, and academically rigorous.  
- [ ] I’ve confirmed that I am using the appropriate academic register and that my document is formatted according to my institution’s requirements.

**Final Review:**

- [ ] I've re-read the proposal to ensure it provides a compelling and coherent argument for the proposed research.  
- [ ] I've considered feedback from my supervisor or peers and made any necessary revisions.  
- [ ] I’ve checked my document for plagiarism and AI-generated text (if applicable).
